Problem Statement

Female second-year CS/ICT students in Cambodia struggle with low confidence and unclear career direction because they lack structured roadmaps, mentorship, and practical career support. As a result, they may fall behind in technical skills, lose motivation, and feel unprepared for the technology industry.

Why It Matters

  • Women are still underrepresented in ICT education and careers.
  • Many students do not know which skills to learn first.
  • Limited guidance can make technology feel difficult or unreachable.
  • Without support, fewer young women may choose or continue in ICT.

Our Solution

SheTech Roadmap brings the full student support process into one professional platform: registration, survey, skill assessment, roaSheTechcontent, mentorship, project tracking, career guidance, and feedback.

1

Assess

Students complete a survey and skill assessment to identify their current level, challenges, confidence, and career interests.

2

Guide

The platform suggests a clear learning roadmap with weekly training modules, resources, and practical tasks.

3

Support

Students receive mentorship, project feedback, community support, and career preparation for internships and future jobs.

Platform Features

The website is designed as a central support system so students do not need to search randomly or learn alone.

A

Registration & Survey

Collect student background, challenges, learning needs, and career goals.

B

Skill Assessment

Check programming, web development, database, GitHub, English, and confidence level.

C

Personal Roadmap

Recommend learning paths such as beginner, web development, database, UI/UX, or career preparation.

D

Training Modules

Provide weekly lessons, notes, exercises, and resources inside the platform.

E

Mentorship

Connect students with senior students, lecturers, or women working in technology.

F

Project Tracker

Help students complete hands-on projects and build portfolio evidence.
